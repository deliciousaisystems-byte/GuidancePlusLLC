NY Paid Family Leave (PFL)

State-mandated leave insurance that provides employees with paid time off for bonding with a new child, caring for a seriously ill family member, or qualifying military exigencies.

  • Up to 12 weeks of paid leave per year
  • 67% of NY's statewide average weekly wage
  • Funded entirely through employee payroll deduction
  • No cost to the employer
  • Covers bonding, family care, and military events

NY Statutory Disability (DBL)

Short-term disability insurance that replaces a portion of an employee's wages when they're unable to work due to a non-work-related illness, injury, or pregnancy.

  • Up to 26 weeks of benefits
  • 50% of the employee's average weekly wage
  • Maximum of $170/week (statutory minimum)
  • Can be enhanced with supplemental coverage
  • Required for all NY employers with 1+ employees

Who Needs This

NY law is clear — and the penalties are real.

If you have employees in New York, this coverage is not optional. Here's who must comply and what's at stake.

1

Employers with 1+ employees

Any business with at least one full-time or part-time employee working in NY must carry DBL coverage.

2

Employees working 20+ hrs/week

PFL is required for all employees working 20 or more hours per week from day one of employment.

3

Employees under 20 hrs/week

After 175 days of employment, even part-time workers under 20 hours must be covered for PFL.

4

Home care & healthcare agencies

With frequent staff changes and mixed schedules, healthcare employers face the highest compliance risk — and benefit most from an expert broker.

Non-compliance costs more than the coverage.

Employers who fail to provide mandatory PFL and DBL coverage in New York face significant financial and legal consequences — including direct liability for all claims.

  • Penalties up to $500 per non-covered employee per year for DBL violations
  • Employer pays all disability benefits out-of-pocket if uninsured
  • Failure to post required PFL notices: $50 fine per employee per violation
  • Stop-work orders from the Workers' Compensation Board
  • Personal liability for business owners in some cases
